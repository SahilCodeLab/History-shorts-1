Very Short Answer Questions (1–50)

  • Q: What are the two main sources of ancient Indian history?
    A: Literary and archaeological sources.
  • Q: What is meant by a literary source?
    A: Written records like texts, manuscripts, and inscriptions.
  • Q: Name two religious literary sources.
    A: Vedas and Puranas.
  • Q: Who wrote the Arthashastra?
    A: Kautilya (Chanakya).
  • Q: What is the importance of the Rigveda?
    A: It is the earliest source of Vedic culture and religion.
  • Q: What are archaeological sources?
    A: Material remains like tools, coins, and inscriptions.
  • Q: Who is known as the father of Indian archaeology?
    A: Alexander Cunningham.
  • Q: What is epigraphy?
    A: The study of inscriptions.
  • Q: What do inscriptions provide?
    A: Information on rulers, administration, and events.
  • Q: What is numismatics?
    A: The study of coins.
  • Q: Name a famous inscription of Ashoka.
    A: Rock Edict XIII.
  • Q: What do coins reveal?
    A: Economy, rulers, religion, and language.
  • Q: What is a manuscript?
    A: Handwritten document, usually on palm leaves or birch bark.
  • Q: Name two foreign travelers to India.
    A: Megasthenes and Fa-Hien.
  • Q: What is the Indica?
    A: A book by Megasthenes on Mauryan India.
  • Q: What is oral tradition?
    A: Passing of history through speech, songs, and stories.
  • Q: What do monuments tell us?
    A: Religious and cultural practices of the past.
  • Q: What is a limitation of literary sources?
    A: They may be biased or exaggerated.
  • Q: What is excavation?
    A: The process of digging to find historical remains.
  • Q: Which source is more reliable – archaeological or literary?
    A: Archaeological, because it is physical evidence.
  • Q: Name a secular literary source.
    A: Rajatarangini by Kalhana.
  • Q: What do Indus Valley seals indicate?
    A: Trade, religion, and script.
  • Q: What is carbon dating used for?
    A: To find the age of organic archaeological remains.
  • Q: What is the use of pottery in archaeology?
    A: It shows lifestyle, trade, and culture.
  • Q: How do foreign accounts help historians?
    A: They provide outside perspectives on Indian society.
  • Q: What is a primary source?
    A: A source created during the actual time of an event.
  • Q: Name one archaeological site in India.
    A: Mohenjo-Daro.
  • Q: What is the meaning of history?
    A: A systematic study of past events.
  • Q: What is proto-history?
    A: Period with archaeological evidence but no writing.
  • Q: What is pre-history?
    A: Time before writing was invented.
  • Q: What is post-history?
    A: Time after writing and proper records became common.
  • Q: Name any two archaeological sources.
    A: Terracotta figures and seals.
  • Q: What is the goal of history writing?
    A: To understand human development through time.
  • Q: What is chronology?
    A: Arrangement of events in the order they happened.
  • Q: What is oral history?
    A: History passed down by word of mouth.
  • Q: What do coins tell us about kings?
    A: Name, dynasty, religion, and economy.
  • Q: What is material culture?
    A: Objects made and used by people of the past.
  • Q: Name a tool used in excavation.
    A: Trowel.
  • Q: What is an inscription?
    A: Writing engraved on stone, metal, or clay.
  • Q: What is the value of Megasthenes’ Indica?
    A: It describes Mauryan society and administration.
  • Q: What are chronicles?
    A: Historical events written in date order.
  • Q: Who wrote Rajatarangini?
    A: Kalhana.
  • Q: What is the difference between mythology and history?
    A: Mythology is based on beliefs; history on facts.
  • Q: Why are sources important in history?
    A: They help reconstruct and understand the past.
  • Q: What is stratigraphy?
    A: Study of soil layers to date archaeological remains.
  • Q: What is paleography?
    A: Study of ancient scripts.
  • Q: What does ASI stand for?
    A: Archaeological Survey of India.
  • Q: Name a limitation of archaeological sources.
    A: They can be incomplete or damaged.
  • Q: How do coins help date events?
    A: They mention the ruler and sometimes the year.
  • Q: What are vernacular texts?
    A: Texts written in regional languages.

Chapter 2: Harappan Civilization – Very Short Answer Questions (1–50)

  • Q: What is another name for Harappan Civilization?
    A: Indus Valley Civilization.
  • Q: When was the Harappan Civilization discovered?
    A: In 1921.
  • Q: Who discovered Harappa?
    A: Daya Ram Sahni.
  • Q: Who discovered Mohenjo-Daro?
    A: R.D. Banerjee.
  • Q: Where is Harappa located now?
    A: In present-day Pakistan (Punjab region).
  • Q: Where is Mohenjo-Daro located?
    A: Sindh, Pakistan.
  • Q: What is the time period of the Harappan Civilization?
    A: Approx. 2500 BCE to 1750 BCE.
  • Q: What type of civilization was it?
    A: Urban civilization.
  • Q: Name two important cities of Harappan Civilization.
    A: Harappa and Mohenjo-Daro.
  • Q: What was the main occupation of Harappan people?
    A: Agriculture.
  • Q: Which crop was mainly grown by Harappans?
    A: Wheat and barley.
  • Q: What was the script used in Harappan Civilization?
    A: Pictographic script.
  • Q: Is the Harappan script deciphered?
    A: No, it is still undeciphered.
  • Q: What were the houses made of?
    A: Baked bricks.
  • Q: What was the main building material?
    A: Burnt bricks.
  • Q: What was the most important public building in Mohenjo-Daro?
    A: The Great Bath.
  • Q: What does the Great Bath suggest?
    A: Ritual bathing or religious activity.
  • Q: Name a famous sculpture found at Mohenjo-Daro.
    A: The Dancing Girl (bronze statue).
  • Q: Name another famous statue from Harappa.
    A: Bearded Priest (stone statue).
  • Q: What is the importance of seals in Harappan culture?
    A: Used for trade and identification.
  • Q: What animal is most commonly seen on Harappan seals?
    A: Unicorn (mythical animal).
  • Q: What were seals made of?
    A: Steatite (soft stone).
  • Q: What were the Harappan tools made of?
    A: Copper and bronze.
  • Q: What kind of economy did Harappans have?
    A: Mixed – agriculture, trade, and crafts.
  • Q: Did Harappans know about iron?
    A: No, iron was unknown to them.
  • Q: Name two crafts practiced by Harappans.
    A: Pottery and bead-making.
  • Q: What kind of pottery did Harappans use?
    A: Red ware with black designs.
  • Q: Which metal was not used by Harappans?
    A: Iron.
  • Q: What were Harappan ornaments made of?
    A: Gold, silver, terracotta, and beads.
  • Q: Did women wear ornaments in Harappan times?
    A: Yes, ornaments for both men and women.
  • Q: What does the uniformity of bricks indicate?
    A: Central planning and strong administration.
  • Q: What kind of drainage system did Harappans have?
    A: Well-planned underground drainage.
  • Q: What were granaries used for?
    A: Storage of food grains.
  • Q: What was the system of weights and measures?
    A: Binary system (1, 2, 4, 8, 16...).
  • Q: What was Lothal famous for?
    A: Its dockyard.
  • Q: Where is Lothal located?
    A: In Gujarat, India.
  • Q: What was found at Kalibangan?
    A: Ploughed fields.
  • Q: What were terracotta toys used for?
    A: Children’s entertainment.
  • Q: What animals were domesticated by Harappans?
    A: Cattle, sheep, goat, and dog.
  • Q: What kind of religion did Harappans follow?
    A: Nature worship and mother goddess worship.
  • Q: Name a religious symbol used by Harappans.
    A: Pipal tree and swastika.
  • Q: Was there any evidence of temples?
    A: No temples found in Harappan sites.
  • Q: What is the meaning of ‘Mohenjo-Daro’?
    A: ‘Mound of the Dead’.
  • Q: How did Harappans dispose of the dead?
    A: Burial was the common method.
  • Q: What kind of burial was used?
    A: Extended and pot burial.
  • Q: What is the likely reason for the decline of the Harappan Civilization?
    A: Floods, climate change, and invasions.
  • Q: What do copper tools suggest about the Harappans?
    A: They were in the Chalcolithic (copper) age.
  • Q: Did Harappans trade with other regions?
    A: Yes, with Mesopotamia and Central Asia.
  • Q: What evidence proves trade with Mesopotamia?
    A: Mesopotamian seals and texts mention ‘Meluha’ (Harappa).
  • Q: What is the legacy of the Harappan Civilization?
    A: Urban planning, drainage, weights, and trade systems.

Chapter 3: Vedic Civilization – Very Short Answer Questions (1–50)

  • Q: What is the time period of Rigvedic civilization?
    A: 1500 BCE – 1000 BCE.
  • Q: What is the time period of Later Vedic civilization?
    A: 1000 BCE – 600 BCE.
  • Q: Who were the Aryans?
    A: Nomadic people who migrated to India from Central Asia.
  • Q: What was the main source of knowledge about the Vedic period?
    A: The Vedas.
  • Q: How many Vedas are there?
    A: Four.
  • Q: Name the four Vedas.
    A: Rigveda, Samaveda, Yajurveda, Atharvaveda.
  • Q: Which is the oldest Veda?
    A: Rigveda.
  • Q: What language were the Vedas written in?
    A: Sanskrit.
  • Q: What does ‘Veda’ mean?
    A: Knowledge.
  • Q: Who were the priests in Vedic society?
    A: Brahmins.
  • Q: What was the main occupation in Vedic period?
    A: Agriculture.
  • Q: What was the unit of society in Rigvedic period?
    A: Family or kula.
  • Q: Who was the head of the family?
    A: Grihapati.
  • Q: What was the head of tribe called?
    A: Rajan.
  • Q: Which assembly selected the king?
    A: Samiti.
  • Q: Name any two assemblies in Vedic period.
    A: Sabha and Samiti.
  • Q: What was Sabha?
    A: Council of elders.
  • Q: What kind of society was Rigvedic society?
    A: Patriarchal.
  • Q: Which metal was used in the Vedic period?
    A: Copper and bronze; later iron.
  • Q: What was the most important animal in the Vedic period?
    A: Cow.
  • Q: What is the term for cow in Rigveda?
    A: Aghanya (not to be killed).
  • Q: Did women get education in the Vedic period?
    A: Yes, especially in Rigvedic period.
  • Q: Name a famous woman scholar of Rigvedic period.
    A: Lopamudra.
  • Q: What was the status of women in Later Vedic period?
    A: It declined compared to early Vedic period.
  • Q: What kind of economy did they follow?
    A: Pastoral and agricultural.
  • Q: Which river is most praised in Rigveda?
    A: River Saraswati.
  • Q: Name the sacred books of Vedic religion.
    A: Vedas and Brahmanas.
  • Q: What was the importance of fire in rituals?
    A: Agni was considered a messenger to gods.
  • Q: What was the primary food in Vedic period?
    A: Milk, ghee, barley, and meat.
  • Q: What were people called who composed hymns?
    A: Rishis.
  • Q: Which god was most important in Rigveda?
    A: Indra.
  • Q: Who was the god of fire?
    A: Agni.
  • Q: Who was the god of wind?
    A: Vayu.
  • Q: Who was the god of sun?
    A: Surya.
  • Q: Which god was known as Purandara (destroyer of forts)?
    A: Indra.
  • Q: What was the position of the king in Later Vedic period?
    A: More powerful than in Rigvedic period.
  • Q: Name four varnas of Vedic society.
    A: Brahmin, Kshatriya, Vaishya, Shudra.
  • Q: Who wrote the Vedic hymns?
    A: The Rishis.
  • Q: What is Purusha Sukta?
    A: A hymn in Rigveda describing origin of varna system.
  • Q: What was ‘Ashvamedha Yajna’?
    A: A horse sacrifice ritual by kings.
  • Q: Name one famous epic developed in Later Vedic period.
    A: Mahabharata or Ramayana.
  • Q: What are Brahmanas?
    A: Commentaries on Vedic rituals.
  • Q: What are Aranyakas?
    A: Forest texts discussing philosophy.
  • Q: What are Upanishads?
    A: Philosophical texts explaining spiritual ideas.
  • Q: What is the term for Vedic schools?
    A: Gurukulas.
  • Q: What was the medium of education?
    A: Oral transmission (Shruti).
  • Q: What was the system of education in Vedic age?
    A: Guru-Shishya tradition.
  • Q: Which occupations were considered impure?
    A: Leather work, butchering, etc.
  • Q: What was the importance of iron in Later Vedic period?
    A: Helped in clearing forests for agriculture.
  • Q: What was the main source of income for the king?
    A: Bali (tax from people).

Chapter 4: Territorial States and the Rise of Magadha – Very Short Answer Questions (1–50)

  • Q: What were Mahajanapadas?
    A: Mahajanapadas were sixteen large territorial states in North India during the 6th century BCE.
  • Q: Name any four Mahajanapadas.
    A: Magadha, Kosala, Vatsa, Avanti.
  • Q: What was the capital of Magadha?
    A: Rajagriha initially, later Pataliputra.
  • Q: Which geographical area did Magadha cover?
    A: Parts of present-day Bihar and eastern Uttar Pradesh.
  • Q: What are Janapadas?
    A: Janapadas were smaller kingdoms before the rise of Mahajanapadas.
  • Q: What helped the emergence of Mahajanapadas?
    A: Iron tools, settled agriculture, and growing trade.
  • Q: How did iron help Mahajanapadas grow?
    A: Iron tools improved agriculture and weapons, strengthening economy and army.
  • Q: Which source mentions Mahajanapadas?
    A: Buddhist text Anguttara Nikaya.
  • Q: What kind of government did Mahajanapadas have?
    A: Some had monarchies, others had republican forms of government.
  • Q: What was the basis of political power in Mahajanapadas?
    A: Control over land, agriculture, and trade routes.
  • Q: What role did rivers play in Magadha’s rise?
    A: Rivers like Ganga and Son aided transport, irrigation, and trade.
  • Q: How did fertile soil contribute to Magadha’s success?
    A: It supported surplus food production, leading to economic strength.
  • Q: What natural resources did Magadha have?
    A: Iron ore, elephants, and forests.
  • Q: Why were elephants important for Magadha?
    A: They strengthened the army in battles.
  • Q: Who was Bimbisara?
    A: An early ruler of Magadha from the Haryanka dynasty who expanded the kingdom.
  • Q: What was Bimbisara’s policy for expansion?
    A: Use of marriage alliances, war, and diplomacy.
  • Q: Name the wives of Bimbisara.
    A: One from Kosala, one from Lichchhavi, and one from Madra.
  • Q: Who was Ajatashatru?
    A: Son of Bimbisara, he expanded Magadha by conquering neighbors.
  • Q: What was Ajatashatru’s major war?
    A: War against the Lichchhavis to annex Vaishali.
  • Q: What was the 'Mahasena' weapon?
    A: A war machine (catapult) used by Ajatashatru in the Lichchhavi war.
  • Q: What was the contribution of Udayin?
    A: He shifted the capital to Pataliputra due to its strategic location.
  • Q: Which dynasties ruled Magadha before Mauryas?
    A: Haryanka, Shishunaga, and Nanda.
  • Q: Who was the founder of the Shishunaga dynasty?
    A: Shishunaga, a minister who overthrew the Haryankas.
  • Q: What was the capital under Shishunaga?
    A: Vaishali, later shifted back to Pataliputra.
  • Q: What is the significance of Pataliputra?
    A: It was a strategic location at the confluence of rivers.
  • Q: Who established the Nanda dynasty?
    A: Mahapadma Nanda.
  • Q: What was Mahapadma Nanda known for?
    A: He extended Magadha’s boundaries and created a powerful army.
  • Q: Why were the Nandas unpopular?
    A: Due to heavy taxation and oppressive rule.
  • Q: What was the role of trade in Magadha’s rise?
    A: Magadha controlled important trade routes and gained wealth.
  • Q: How did Magadha’s army help in its success?
    A: It had a large, well-equipped army with elephants.
  • Q: What kind of tax system did Magadha have?
    A: Agricultural taxes, forest produce taxes, and trade taxes.
  • Q: What was the role of agriculture in Magadha’s growth?
    A: It ensured food security and economic stability.
  • Q: Why was the Ganga plain important?
    A: Its fertile soil and water supply supported dense settlements.
  • Q: How did political alliances aid Magadha’s rise?
    A: Marriages with royal families ensured peace and cooperation.
  • Q: What were Ganasanghas?
    A: Republican states where decisions were made collectively by rulers.
  • Q: Name one Ganasangha state.
    A: Lichchhavis of Vaishali.
  • Q: How did Magadha defeat the Ganasanghas?
    A: Through strong military and political strategy, especially under Ajatashatru.
  • Q: What was the importance of fortification?
    A: Fortified cities like Rajagriha gave military protection.
  • Q: What led to the decline of other Mahajanapadas?
    A: Internal conflicts and defeat by Magadha’s strong army.
  • Q: How did Magadha maintain its power?
    A: By continuous expansion, strong administration, and strategic diplomacy.
  • Q: Who was Dhanananda?
    A: The last Nanda ruler, overthrown by Chandragupta Maurya.
  • Q: What is the significance of Magadha’s location?
    A: It was centrally located with access to rich resources and trade routes.
  • Q: What was the political structure of Magadha?
    A: Monarchical, with a king supported by ministers and army.
  • Q: How did Buddhism spread from Magadha?
    A: Magadha was home to Gautama Buddha and many early followers.
  • Q: What was the role of Brahmins in Magadhan society?
    A: They had a significant religious and advisory role.
  • Q: What role did forests play?
    A: Provided timber, elephants, and refuge during wars.
  • Q: What language was spoken in Magadha?
    A: Magadhi Prakrit.
  • Q: What was the Magadhan coinage like?
    A: Punch-marked silver coins used for trade.
  • Q: Which Greek writer wrote about Magadha?
    A: Megasthenes (though mainly about Mauryan Magadha).
  • Q: Why did Magadha ultimately become a powerful empire?
    A: Due to its geography, leadership, resources, and military strength.

Chapter 5: Iranian and Macedonian Invasions – Very Short Answer Questions (1–50)

  • Q: Who were the Iranians?
    A: Iranians belonged to the Achaemenid Empire of Persia.
  • Q: When did Iranian invasions start in India?
    A: Around 6th century BCE under Darius I.
  • Q: Which region did Iranians conquer in India?
    A: North-Western India, mainly Punjab and Sindh.
  • Q: Who was the first Achaemenid ruler to invade India?
    A: Darius I.
  • Q: Why was India attractive to Iranians?
    A: Due to its wealth and fertile land.
  • Q: What did Herodotus write about Indian tribute?
    A: He stated India paid the highest tribute to Persia.
  • Q: What administrative system did Persians bring?
    A: The Satrap system (provincial administration).
  • Q: What was a ‘Satrap’?
    A: A governor of a province in Persian administration.
  • Q: What cultural influence did Iran leave on India?
    A: Art, architecture, coinage, and royal customs.
  • Q: Name the Persian emperor who ruled India after Darius.
    A: Xerxes.
  • Q: How long did Persian rule last in India?
    A: About 200 years.
  • Q: What art form was influenced by Persian styles?
    A: Ashokan pillars and Mauryan art show Persian influence.
  • Q: Who was Alexander the Great?
    A: A Macedonian king who invaded India in 326 BCE.
  • Q: Why did Alexander invade India?
    A: To expand his empire and find the edge of the world.
  • Q: Which river did Alexander cross to enter India?
    A: The Indus River.
  • Q: Which Indian king did Alexander fight?
    A: King Porus (Puru) of the Punjab region.
  • Q: Where did Alexander fight Porus?
    A: At the Battle of Hydaspes (River Jhelum), 326 BCE.
  • Q: What happened after the battle with Porus?
    A: Alexander won but made Porus his ally and reinstated him.
  • Q: Why did Alexander stop his conquest?
    A: His soldiers refused to go further due to fear and exhaustion.
  • Q: Where did Alexander's army turn back?
    A: At the river Beas.
  • Q: How did Alexander die?
    A: In 323 BCE at Babylon due to illness.
  • Q: What was the impact of Alexander’s invasion?
    A: It opened up trade and cultural links between India and the West.
  • Q: Did Alexander conquer the whole of India?
    A: No, he only conquered parts of the north-west.
  • Q: What happened to Indian territories after Alexander?
    A: They were ruled by his generals, especially Seleucus Nicator.
  • Q: How did Alexander’s invasion help Chandragupta Maurya?
    A: It weakened local rulers, allowing Chandragupta to rise.
  • Q: Who were the Indo-Greeks?
    A: Descendants of Alexander’s generals who ruled parts of India.
  • Q: What did Alexander learn from Indian culture?
    A: Philosophy, religious tolerance, and warfare techniques.
  • Q: Name a Greek historian who accompanied Alexander.
    A: Arrian and Megasthenes (later under Mauryas).
  • Q: What was the Greek impact on Indian coinage?
    A: Use of portrait coins and Hellenistic designs.
  • Q: How did Alexander treat defeated kings?
    A: He often reinstated them as vassals.
  • Q: Which regions came under Alexander’s control in India?
    A: Punjab, Sindh, and some parts of present-day Afghanistan.
  • Q: How did Indian soldiers respond to Alexander?
    A: They fiercely resisted, especially under Porus.
  • Q: What did Alexander establish in India?
    A: Cities like Alexandria on the Indus (now in Pakistan).
  • Q: What was Alexander's strategy in India?
    A: Divide and rule by making alliances with some rulers.
  • Q: How many battles did Alexander fight in India?
    A: Mainly one major battle—Hydaspes.
  • Q: Who ruled after Alexander’s death in India?
    A: His general Seleucus, later defeated by Chandragupta.
  • Q: What religion did Alexander’s soldiers encounter in India?
    A: Hinduism, Jainism, and early forms of Buddhism.
  • Q: What impact did Alexander's invasion have on trade?
    A: Trade routes between India and West Asia increased.
  • Q: What was Alexander's impression of Porus?
    A: He admired Porus for his bravery and dignity.
  • Q: Did Alexander’s invasion lead to permanent control?
    A: No, his rule was short-lived and lacked deep influence.
  • Q: What architectural elements came from Persians and Greeks?
    A: Pillars, capitals, and realistic human sculptures.
  • Q: What is Greco-Buddhist art?
    A: Fusion of Greek and Buddhist styles seen in Gandhara.
  • Q: What was the economic impact of the invasions?
    A: Improved trade, coin usage, and administrative systems.
  • Q: What writing system did the Persians introduce?
    A: Aramaic script influenced Indian writing styles.
  • Q: How did Greek art influence Indian sculpture?
    A: Naturalistic body forms and detailed faces.
  • Q: How were Indian cities affected by invasions?
    A: Some were destroyed, others rebuilt with new influences.
  • Q: What was the legacy of Alexander in India?
    A: Cultural exchange and military tactics.
  • Q: Did Alexander marry in India?
    A: Yes, he married Roxana from Bactria, not an Indian princess.
  • Q: How did Indian kings react to foreign invasions?
    A: Some resisted, others allied for survival.
  • Q: What was the biggest long-term effect of these invasions?
    A: Greater Indo-Western interaction in art, trade, and politics.

Chapter 6: Emergence of New Religious Movements: Buddhism and Jainism – Very Short Answer Questions (1–50)

  • Q: What was the main reason behind the rise of new religious movements in the 6th century BCE?
    A: The growing dominance of Brahmanism, rituals, and social inequality led to dissatisfaction, resulting in the rise of Buddhism and Jainism.
  • Q: Name the two major religious reformers of the 6th century BCE.
    A: Gautama Buddha and Vardhamana Mahavira.
  • Q: Who was the founder of Buddhism?
    A: Siddhartha Gautama, known as the Buddha, founded Buddhism.
  • Q: Who founded Jainism?
    A: Jainism was founded by Vardhamana Mahavira, the 24th Tirthankara.
  • Q: What does ‘Buddha’ mean?
    A: ‘Buddha’ means ‘The Enlightened One’ or ‘The Awakened One’.
  • Q: Where was Buddha born?
    A: Buddha was born at Lumbini (in present-day Nepal).
  • Q: What was Buddha’s early life like?
    A: He was born in a royal family of the Shakya clan and lived a luxurious life before renouncing the world.
  • Q: What event led Buddha to renounce worldly life?
    A: Seeing an old man, a sick man, a dead body, and a monk inspired him to seek truth.
  • Q: What is the Great Renunciation?
    A: When Siddhartha left his palace and family to search for enlightenment.
  • Q: Where did Buddha attain enlightenment?
    A: Under the Bodhi tree at Bodh Gaya.
  • Q: What is the first sermon of Buddha called?
    A: Dharmachakra Pravartana (Turning the Wheel of Dharma), delivered at Sarnath.
  • Q: What are the Four Noble Truths in Buddhism?
    A: Life is suffering; suffering is caused by desire; ending desire ends suffering; follow the Eightfold Path to end suffering.
  • Q: What is the Eightfold Path?
    A: Right view, intention, speech, action, livelihood, effort, mindfulness, and concentration.
  • Q: What is Nirvana?
    A: Liberation from the cycle of birth and death (moksha).
  • Q: Where did Buddha die?
    A: Kushinagar (in present-day Uttar Pradesh).
  • Q: What is the Tripitaka?
    A: It is the sacred book of Buddhism, containing the teachings of Buddha.
  • Q: What are the main sects of Buddhism?
    A: Hinayana and Mahayana.
  • Q: What does Mahayana focus on?
    A: Worship of Buddha as a god and belief in Bodhisattvas.
  • Q: What is Hinayana?
    A: A sect that follows the original teachings of Buddha and sees him as a teacher, not a god.
  • Q: Who was Mahavira?
    A: The 24th Tirthankara of Jainism who gave it its present form.
  • Q: Where was Mahavira born?
    A: He was born at Kundagrama near Vaishali.
  • Q: What was Mahavira’s early life like?
    A: He was a prince who left his home at 30 to attain spiritual knowledge.
  • Q: How long did Mahavira meditate?
    A: He meditated for 12 years before attaining Kevalya (knowledge).
  • Q: What is Kevalya?
    A: Complete knowledge or supreme wisdom in Jainism.
  • Q: What are the five great vows in Jainism?
    A: Non-violence, truth, non-stealing, celibacy, and non-possession.
  • Q: What is Ahimsa?
    A: The principle of non-violence towards all living beings.
  • Q: What is the symbol of Jainism?
    A: The hand with a wheel (Ahimsa hand).
  • Q: What is the sacred book of Jainism?
    A: Agamas.
  • Q: Name the two sects of Jainism.
    A: Shvetambaras and Digambaras.
  • Q: What is the difference between Shvetambaras and Digambaras?
    A: Shvetambaras wear white clothes; Digambaras remain nude.
  • Q: Where did Mahavira die?
    A: Pawapuri, Bihar.
  • Q: What is the importance of Sarnath in Buddhism?
    A: It’s where Buddha gave his first sermon.
  • Q: What was Buddha’s teaching on caste?
    A: Buddha rejected caste-based discrimination.
  • Q: What is the Sangha in Buddhism?
    A: A community of monks and nuns who followed Buddha’s path.
  • Q: Who supported Buddhism during Buddha’s life?
    A: Kings like Bimbisara and followers like Anathapindika.
  • Q: What is the doctrine of Karma in Buddhism?
    A: Actions determine future rebirths.
  • Q: What does Jainism say about the soul?
    A: Each soul is eternal and responsible for its actions.
  • Q: How did Buddhism spread?
    A: Through missionaries, kings like Ashoka, and trade routes.
  • Q: What role did Ashoka play in Buddhism?
    A: He embraced Buddhism and spread it across Asia.
  • Q: What were Buddhist monasteries called?
    A: Viharas.
  • Q: What were the reasons for Buddhism’s decline in India?
    A: Revival of Hinduism, loss of royal support, and internal divisions.
  • Q: What is the meaning of ‘Tirthankara’?
    A: Spiritual teacher who shows the path of liberation.
  • Q: What is the Jain concept of Moksha?
    A: Freedom from birth-death cycle achieved through truth and penance.
  • Q: Why did Jainism not spread widely?
    A: Strict rules like complete non-violence and difficult lifestyle.
  • Q: What is the meaning of ‘Jina’?
    A: Conqueror of desires and passions.
  • Q: What was Buddha’s view on God?
    A: He did not speak of God but focused on ethical living and self-discipline.
  • Q: How were Buddhist councils important?
    A: They helped compile and preserve Buddha’s teachings.
  • Q: Name one foreign traveller who wrote about Buddhism.
    A: Fa-Hien (China).
  • Q: What is the importance of Bodh Gaya?
    A: Place where Buddha attained enlightenment.
  • Q: What is the core similarity between Buddhism and Jainism?
    A: Both rejected Vedas, caste system, and emphasized non-violence and self-realization.

Chapter 7: Emergence and Growth of the Mauryan Empire – Administration, Economy, Ashoka's Dhamma, Art & Architecture – Very Short Answer Questions (1–50)

  • Q: Who founded the Mauryan Empire?
    A: Chandragupta Maurya founded the Mauryan Empire in 321 BCE.
  • Q: Who helped Chandragupta in building the empire?
    A: Chanakya (Kautilya), a Brahmin advisor and economist.
  • Q: Which book did Chanakya write?
    A: He wrote the Arthashastra, a text on politics and economy.
  • Q: What was the capital of the Mauryan Empire?
    A: Pataliputra (modern-day Patna).
  • Q: Who succeeded Chandragupta Maurya?
    A: His son Bindusara.
  • Q: Who was the most famous Mauryan ruler?
    A: Ashoka the Great.
  • Q: Which battle changed Ashoka’s life?
    A: The Kalinga War in 261 BCE.
  • Q: What happened after the Kalinga War?
    A: Ashoka embraced Buddhism and non-violence.
  • Q: What is Ashoka’s Dhamma?
    A: Moral teachings based on compassion, truth, and respect.
  • Q: In which language were Ashokan edicts written?
    A: Mostly in Prakrit using Brahmi script.
  • Q: What are rock edicts?
    A: Ashoka’s teachings engraved on rocks and pillars.
  • Q: What was the extent of the Mauryan Empire?
    A: From Afghanistan in the northwest to Bengal and Karnataka.
  • Q: Who was the Greek ambassador in Chandragupta's court?
    A: Megasthenes.
  • Q: What book did Megasthenes write?
    A: Indica, describing Mauryan administration and society.
  • Q: Who controlled the central government in the Mauryan Empire?
    A: The emperor with ministers and officials.
  • Q: What was the role of spies in Mauryan administration?
    A: To gather intelligence and maintain internal security.
  • Q: What kind of army did the Mauryas have?
    A: A large standing army with infantry, cavalry, elephants, and chariots.
  • Q: Who was the head of city administration?
    A: Nagaradhyaksha (City Superintendent).
  • Q: How were provinces governed?
    A: By royal princes or governors called Kumaras.
  • Q: What type of economy did the Mauryas follow?
    A: Agricultural with state control over trade and mining.
  • Q: What were the main sources of revenue?
    A: Land tax, trade tax, and tolls.
  • Q: What is Ashoka’s major contribution to Indian history?
    A: Spread of Buddhism and moral governance (Dhamma).
  • Q: What was the role of Buddhism in Ashoka’s rule?
    A: He promoted peace, religious tolerance, and welfare.
  • Q: What did Ashoka build to spread Buddhism?
    A: Stupas, viharas, and pillars with inscriptions.
  • Q: Name the famous pillars of Ashoka.
    A: Sarnath, Lauriya Nandangarh, and Allahabad pillars.
  • Q: What is the Lion Capital of Ashoka?
    A: The national emblem of India, found at Sarnath.
  • Q: What was the Mauryan contribution to art?
    A: Stone architecture, polished pillars, and rock-cut caves.
  • Q: Name a famous stupa from Mauryan times.
    A: The Sanchi Stupa.
  • Q: What is Mauryan polish?
    A: High-gloss finish on stone structures, unique to the period.
  • Q: How was trade conducted in Mauryan India?
    A: Through roads, ports, and government-regulated markets.
  • Q: What was the role of the state in economy?
    A: State owned forests, mines, and regulated trade.
  • Q: What are minor rock edicts?
    A: Short inscriptions by Ashoka on local stones or rocks.
  • Q: What was Ashoka’s message in edicts?
    A: Non-violence, respect for elders, religious tolerance, and welfare.
  • Q: What were Dhamma Mahamattas?
    A: Special officers appointed to spread Dhamma and look after welfare.
  • Q: How did Ashoka promote religious tolerance?
    A: By respecting all religions and urging harmony.
  • Q: Where was the third Buddhist Council held?
    A: At Pataliputra under Ashoka's patronage.
  • Q: What was the significance of the Third Council?
    A: Reorganization of the Buddhist Sangha and sending of missionaries.
  • Q: Name two countries where Ashoka sent Buddhist missions.
    A: Sri Lanka and Myanmar.
  • Q: Who led the mission to Sri Lanka?
    A: Ashoka’s son Mahinda and daughter Sanghamitta.
  • Q: What happened to the Mauryan Empire after Ashoka?
    A: It declined due to weak successors.
  • Q: Who was the last Mauryan ruler?
    A: Brihadratha Maurya, killed by Pushyamitra Shunga.
  • Q: What is a stupa?
    A: A dome-shaped Buddhist structure containing relics.
  • Q: Name a cave site from Mauryan period.
    A: Barabar Caves in Bihar.
  • Q: What are inscriptions?
    A: Written records on stones or metals issued by kings.
  • Q: What kind of weights and measures were used?
    A: Standardized systems controlled by the state.
  • Q: What was the significance of Ashoka’s edicts?
    A: They give direct evidence of his rule and thoughts.
  • Q: Why did Ashoka stop warfare?
    A: He was deeply affected by the bloodshed at Kalinga.
  • Q: What did Ashoka call himself in the edicts?
    A: Devanampiya Piyadasi (Beloved of the Gods).
  • Q: How did Mauryan rule differ from earlier kingdoms?
    A: It was centralized with an efficient bureaucracy.
  • Q: What is the legacy of the Mauryan Empire?
    A: Strong central administration, ethical rule, and Buddhist spread.

Chapter 8: The Satavahana Phase and The Age of Shakas, Parthians, and Kushans – Very Short Answer Questions (1–50)

  • Q: Who founded the Satavahana dynasty?
    A: Simuka was the founder of the Satavahana dynasty around the 1st century BCE.
  • Q: What was the capital of the Satavahanas?
    A: Pratishthana (modern Paithan in Maharashtra).
  • Q: Which ruler is known as the greatest Satavahana king?
    A: Gautamiputra Satakarni.
  • Q: Who was Gautamiputra Satakarni's mother?
    A: Gautami Balashri, who praised him in the Nashik inscription.
  • Q: Who were the main enemies of the Satavahanas?
    A: The Shakas and Kshatrapas.
  • Q: What title did Gautamiputra Satakarni use?
    A: "Ekabrahmana" and "Shakavansha-shatriya."
  • Q: What was the Satavahana administrative structure?
    A: They had centralized power with ministers and governors.
  • Q: What were ‘Amatyas’ and ‘Maha-matras’?
    A: Officials responsible for administration and revenue.
  • Q: What was the land grant system under Satavahanas?
    A: Land was granted to Brahmins and religious institutions.
  • Q: What language did Satavahanas promote?
    A: Prakrit, especially in inscriptions.
  • Q: What script did they use?
    A: Brahmi script.
  • Q: What religion did Satavahanas follow?
    A: They supported both Brahmanism and Buddhism.
  • Q: Which Buddhist site was developed during their reign?
    A: Amaravati and Sanchi Stupas.
  • Q: What were 'gatas' and 'kulas'?
    A: Social and guild-based community systems.
  • Q: Did women play a role in religion or society?
    A: Yes, royal women like queens made donations to Buddhist sites.
  • Q: What was their main source of revenue?
    A: Land tax and trade.
  • Q: What kind of coins did Satavahanas issue?
    A: Lead, copper, and silver coins with bilingual legends.
  • Q: What was their contribution to Indian art?
    A: Early Buddhist rock-cut architecture and sculptures.
  • Q: How was trade during Satavahana rule?
    A: Flourished through inland and maritime routes.
  • Q: Name two important ports of Satavahana period.
    A: Kalyan and Bharuch.
  • Q: Who succeeded Gautamiputra Satakarni?
    A: Vasisthiputra Pulumavi.
  • Q: What is the Nashik inscription?
    A: It describes Gautamiputra’s achievements, written by his mother.
  • Q: What was the role of guilds (Shrenis)?
    A: Managed trade and crafts, helped regulate economy.
  • Q: What were Buddhist Chaityas and Viharas?
    A: Places of worship and residence for monks.
  • Q: How did Satavahanas maintain political control?
    A: Strong military, local administration, and loyalty of officials.
  • Q: Who were the Shakas?
    A: Central Asian nomadic tribes who entered India after Indo-Greeks.
  • Q: Who was the most famous Shaka ruler?
    A: Rudradaman I.
  • Q: What is Rudradaman famous for?
    A: His Junagadh inscription in Sanskrit.
  • Q: What was the title used by Shaka rulers?
    A: Mahakshatrapa (Great Satrap).
  • Q: Where did the Shakas rule mainly?
    A: Western India – Gujarat, Maharashtra, and Malwa.
  • Q: Who were the Parthians?
    A: Iranians who ruled parts of north-western India.
  • Q: Name a famous Parthian ruler.
    A: Gondophernes, mentioned in Christian texts.
  • Q: Who were the Kushans?
    A: Central Asian tribe originally from the Yueh-Chih.
  • Q: Who was the most famous Kushan ruler?
    A: Kanishka the Great.
  • Q: What was the capital of Kushan Empire?
    A: Peshawar and Mathura.
  • Q: Which era did Kanishka start?
    A: Saka Era in 78 CE.
  • Q: What religion did Kanishka follow?
    A: Initially Shaivism, later Buddhism.
  • Q: What is the importance of the Fourth Buddhist Council?
    A: Held by Kanishka in Kashmir to codify Buddhist texts.
  • Q: What script was used in Kushan coins?
    A: Bactrian (Greek script).
  • Q: What was unique about Kushan coins?
    A: Featured Indian and foreign deities like Shiva, Buddha, and Greek gods.
  • Q: What was the Gandhara Art?
    A: A blend of Greek and Indian art, flourished under Kushans.
  • Q: What is Mathura school of art?
    A: Indigenous art style, known for red sandstone statues.
  • Q: What language was used in inscriptions by Kushans?
    A: Bilingual – Greek and Prakrit.
  • Q: How did Kushans contribute to trade?
    A: They promoted Silk Route trade connecting India to Rome and China.
  • Q: What were the main items of trade during Kushan rule?
    A: Silk, spices, ivory, gems, and textiles.
  • Q: What kind of administration did the Kushans have?
    A: Monarchy with semi-autonomous governors.
  • Q: What was society like during Shaka-Kushan period?
    A: Urban-based, multicultural, and religiously tolerant.
  • Q: What was the economic status under Kushans?
    A: Highly prosperous with international trade.
  • Q: How did Kushans promote Buddhism?
    A: Built stupas and patronized monks and missionaries.
  • Q: What was the legacy of these foreign rulers in India?
    A: Cultural fusion, art styles (Gandhara), and expanded trade networks.

Chapter 9: The Sangam Age – Sangam Literature, The Three Early Kingdoms – Very Short Answer Questions (1–50)

  • Q: What is the Sangam Age?
    A: It refers to the period of early Tamil history (300 BCE – 300 CE) when ancient Tamil literature was composed.
  • Q: What does "Sangam" mean?
    A: Sangam means a literary assembly or gathering of poets and scholars.
  • Q: How many Sangams were there?
    A: Three Sangams – only the third is historically verifiable.
  • Q: Where was the third Sangam held?
    A: At Madurai, under the patronage of Pandya kings.
  • Q: What language was Sangam literature written in?
    A: Ancient Tamil.
  • Q: What is the most famous Sangam text?
    A: Tolkappiyam – a grammar book and one of the oldest Tamil texts.
  • Q: What are the two major themes of Sangam literature?
    A: Akam (love/inner life) and Puram (war/public life).
  • Q: What is Ettuthokai?
    A: An anthology of eight collections of poetry.
  • Q: What is Pathupattu?
    A: A collection of ten long poems.
  • Q: What is Purananuru?
    A: A Puram text with war poems and kingly virtues.
  • Q: What is Ainkurunuru?
    A: Akam poetry collection focusing on love themes.
  • Q: What was the main religion in Sangam Age?
    A: Ancestor worship, nature worship, Jainism and some early Hindu beliefs.
  • Q: What is the importance of Sangam literature?
    A: It gives authentic details about Tamil society, polity, economy, and culture.
  • Q: What were Sangam poets called?
    A: They were known as Pulavars.
  • Q: Which profession is frequently mentioned in Sangam texts?
    A: Agriculture and warfare.
  • Q: What type of society was shown in Sangam literature?
    A: Tribal, clan-based with clear roles for kings, warriors, and poets.
  • Q: Was trade discussed in Sangam texts?
    A: Yes, both inland and foreign trade (with Romans) are mentioned.
  • Q: Were women part of Sangam literature?
    A: Yes, many poems were composed by women poets.
  • Q: What was the role of bards and poets?
    A: They praised kings, narrated heroism, and were rewarded.
  • Q: What does Sangam literature reveal about Tamil culture?
    A: It shows a rich and developed culture with arts, warfare, and trade.
  • Q: Name the three early Tamil kingdoms.
    A: Chera, Chola, and Pandya.
  • Q: Where were the Cheras located?
    A: Modern Kerala and western Tamil Nadu.
  • Q: What was the capital of the Cheras?
    A: Vanji (Karur).
  • Q: What was the Cheras' symbol?
    A: The bow and arrow.
  • Q: What port was important for the Cheras?
    A: Muziris – famous for Indo-Roman trade.
  • Q: Name a famous Chera king.
    A: Senguttuvan – known for Silappadikaram and Pattini cult.
  • Q: What religion did Cheras follow?
    A: Initially tribal beliefs, later Jainism and early Shaivism.
  • Q: Where did the Cholas rule?
    A: Along the Cauvery delta, in present-day Tamil Nadu.
  • Q: What was the Chola capital?
    A: Urayur and Puhar (Kaveripattinam).
  • Q: What was the Chola emblem?
    A: Tiger.
  • Q: Name a famous early Chola king.
    A: Karikala Chola – built dams and promoted trade.
  • Q: What was Chola’s economic activity?
    A: Agriculture and maritime trade.
  • Q: What was special about Chola navy?
    A: Strong navy enabled overseas trade and influence.
  • Q: Which Sangam work mentions Karikala?
    A: Pattinappalai.
  • Q: Where did the Pandyas rule?
    A: Southern Tamil Nadu, with Madurai as their capital.
  • Q: What was the emblem of Pandyas?
    A: Fish.
  • Q: What was the Pandya kingdom known for?
    A: Support for literature and trade.
  • Q: What was the role of Pandya kings in Sangam?
    A: They hosted the 3rd Sangam at Madurai.
  • Q: Name a Pandya ruler mentioned in literature.
    A: Nedunjeliyan.
  • Q: What religion did the Pandyas follow?
    A: Initially nature worship; later Hinduism and Jainism.
  • Q: Were the kingdoms democratic?
    A: No, they were hereditary monarchies.
  • Q: How was the army structured?
    A: Included infantry, cavalry, elephants, and chariots.
  • Q: What role did women play in society?
    A: Respected in society; some women poets were also popular.
  • Q: What were trade goods of the Sangam period?
    A: Spices, pearls, ivory, textiles, and precious stones.
  • Q: What foreign power did Tamil kingdoms trade with?
    A: Romans and Greeks.
  • Q: What was the role of Kaveripattinam?
    A: A major port city for overseas trade.
  • Q: How were temples during Sangam age?
    A: Small shrines, not large temples as in later periods.
  • Q: What was the main mode of entertainment?
    A: Music, dance, poetry, and festivals.
  • Q: What were “Velir” chieftains?
    A: Minor kings or nobles who ruled small territories.
  • Q: What is the legacy of Sangam Age?
    A: It laid the foundation for Tamil culture, literature, and regional identity.
